Every Run by Temperature

Blue = cold (<40°F), Green = ideal (40-60°F), Yellow = warm (60-80°F), Red = hot (80°F+)
The sweet spot is 40-45°F with an average pace of 8:41/mi. But the remarkable thing is how flat the curve stays — only 33 seconds separate the fastest and slowest temperature buckets across a 75°F range. Arizona heat adaptation is real: 100°F runs are only 30 seconds slower than 45°F runs.

8-Week Training Load vs Race Pace

The relationship is messy — and that's the insight. Short races cluster fast regardless of training load. 100-milers need 500+ miles of 8-week base, but pace is governed by terrain, not volume. The sweet spot for peak performance appears around 400-600 miles of 8-week training load.
