Method & Caveats

How “track” was detected

For each run's GPS stream, a 90-second sliding window flags points where the bounding box stays under ~220m (track-sized). Adjacent flagged points become a candidate segment, which only counts if it covers ≥1.2 km, lasts ≥4 min, sustains ≥2.6 m/s (~10:20/mi), and contains ≥3 full angular revolutions around the segment centroid (counted as max of clockwise vs. counter-clockwise sweep, so direction-changing workouts still count).

What it catches

Real 400m track sessions (warm-ups, intervals, long efforts), 24-hour track races, and short-loop course races where the loop fits in the bbox window. The lap-closure check kills false positives from slow mountain trail running where GPS clusters tightly during steep climbs.

Manual overrides

Two activities are credited beyond what GPS auto-detect would catch. Six Days in the Dome 100mi (Jun 19, 200m indoor) — no usable GPS. Hotfoot Hamster 6h (Aug 29, 50mi paved short loop) — auto-detect only flagged ~24mi because the loop slightly exceeds the 220m bbox window, but the full distance is functionally lap-running specificity. Treadmill runs are counted as off-track with reported distance.

Why 13.4%, not higher

Track race days inflate the number, but the bulk of buildup training was trail and road. The dedicated track block — Oct 31 (25 mi), Nov 7 (62 mi), Nov 27 (29 mi), Dec 4 (17 mi) — added ~133 mi of focused lap work in the final 6 weeks, on top of an Apr 24 30 mi solo session and the May/March race-pace and June Dome sessions.
